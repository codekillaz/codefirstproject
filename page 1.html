Cheap Food & Drink

There are plenty of cheap food and drink options out there.

  1. Do some research and have a plan before you leave
  2. Bringing your own tea and coffee saves you lots of money(make sure you also take a thermos with you)
  3. You can also bring a knife(not in the backpack) and a chopping board, so that you can cut the costs of food by cooking using products from the grocery store- bring your own cutlery
  4. Search beforehand for cheap supermarkets in the area( Do as the Locals Do)
  5. Find Accommodations with a kitchen
  6. Drink tab water only if you researched the fact that it is drinkable
  7. Find the best food deals if you want to eat out
  8. Eat The Free Breakfast
  9. GO FOR THE HAPPY HOUR- many restaurants have huge discounts or free meals during lunch time

Cheap Accommodation

There is a wide range of affordable accommodation available for all budget types.

  1. Create an account on couchsurfing if you are confident enough in spending the night for free in a stranger’s home
  2. Airbnb is a great opportunity to get to know the culture of a place
  3. Hostels are another cheap alternative
  4. Use your network and start contacting people who you have met and who can provide you accommodation
  5. If the place where you are going has favourable weather you can try camping, that can save you lots of money

Cheap Travel

The best travel options presented that are both time efficient and cheap.

  1. Keep an eye on the Ryanair ,easyJet and Interrrail for cheap flights around Europe
  2. It’s never too late to start hitch hiking, but I do not recommend it to girls who travel alone, as it might get quite dangerous
  3. Search beforehand for different transportation around the area and check what are the best deals
  4. Join different memberships which discount your flights (such as credit cards, clubs and organisations)
  5. Take Advantage of Your Hotel- search for what is free and included in the travel package
  6. Volunteer / Work Exchange. There are so many opportunities in these areas

Cheap Thrills

We’ve picked out the fun things to do without busting the budget and keep an eye out on the best deals and discounted rates.

  1. Daily Deal Websites which are food and entertainment daily deal sites, such as Groupon and LivingSocial. You are given a limited amount of time to buy each deal, which may be an excellent discount (as much as 50% off). However, be sure to check the expiration dates. Many deals are good for several months, but others can be very short.
  2. Vendors and Restaurants That Offer Birthday Deals! If you can provide proof, some restaurants offer free dinners or desserts on your birthday, or even at any point during the entire week or month of your birth. You can find birthday deals in your area by going to FreeBirthdayMeals.org and then clicking on your city and state.
  3. University Performing Arts Centers have university productions, concerts, and recitals that you can attend for a fraction of the cost of a Broadway show or professional performance. Also, local theater and choral groups put on shows throughout the year, especially around the holidays. Check your local paper for upcoming events.
  4. Free Events with plenty of activities that are fun and free no matter where you live. In addition to bike riding, hiking, or going to a park, consider these diversions:
    • Free weekday concerts during summertime
    • Free live entertainment at an outdoor venue
    • Smaller festivals and fairs, which often have free admission
    • A day at the beach or local municipal swimming pool
    • Local library special events
    • Outdoor movies at the local park
