It’s easy to lose track of how much you’re spending at the best of times, and when you’re travelling and using different currencies, you’re at risk of losing the plot completely. If you’re savvy, you can time buying foreign currency when the exchange rate is in your favour (i.e. when your currency is more expensive than the other currency).

But if you’re travelling last minute, or you don’t have time to track exchange rates, the best way to limit your spending abroad is to get a pre-loaded foreign money card. There are plenty of companies who can provide you with one, and there are several advantages to consider:

  • It limits your spending from the outset: You start out with a fixed budget, so you’re less tempted to go overboard.
  • Reduced risk: If you lose a prepaid card, or if it’s stolen, you don’t run the risk of your whole bank account being cleaned out.
  • Locked exchange rate: If the exchange rate changes while you’re travelling, these prepaid cards won’t be affected.

Other things to consider:

  • There are lots of places that won’t accept cards, especially in smaller towns, so even with a prepaid card, it’s a good idea to always have some cash to hand.
  • If there’s an unforeseen emergency, like you need to book an extra night’s stay, or change a flight, you need to have access to your bank account. So always make sure you have your card details with you, even if you don’t take your card.
